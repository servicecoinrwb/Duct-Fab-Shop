MT SKETCH — HOW THIS WORKS

A quick markup board for site surveys and quote sketches. Grid squares are 1 ft nominal, so duct widths draw roughly to scale.

TOOLS

V Select/Move · H Pan · P Pen · L Line · B Box · D Duct run · T Text · E Eraser

DUCT RUNS (STRAIGHT DUCT & PIPE)

You don't stamp straight sections — the Duct run tool draws them. Click each corner along the run; elbows form automatically at every turn. Pick Rect (mitered square elbows) or Round (radiused pipe elbows) and set the width/diameter in inches in the top bar. Enter or double-click finishes, Esc cancels. The palette fittings are for the pieces that sit on or between runs: takeoffs, wyes, reducers, dampers, boots.

LAYERS

Existing (sky) for what's there now, Proposed (rust) for new work, Notes (cream) for dimensions and callouts. The eye icon hides a layer without deleting anything.

SELECTION

Click an object with Select, then drag to move. Q/W rotate symbols, F flips/mirrors them (for opposite-hand elbows, wyes, offsets), [/] resize, Ctrl+D duplicate, Del delete.

VIEW & FILES

Scroll wheel zooms at the cursor; middle-drag or hold Space to pan. Ctrl+Z/Y undo/redo. The board autosaves in this browser; Save/Open handles .json project files. PNG ⤓ exports the current view as-is; PDF ⤓ auto-fits the whole drawing onto letter landscape with a white background and the MT title block — that's the one to attach to quotes or hand to a customer.
